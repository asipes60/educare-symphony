# EduCare NBCC Course Auditor

Performs a complete NBCC ACEP Home Study compliance audit on any EduCare continuing education course. All checklist items are grounded in the **NBCC Continuing Education Provider Policy** (Board approved November 8, 2014) and **NBCC Tips for Designing a Home Study Program** (© 2024 NBCC). Produces a structured gap analysis with section-by-section word counts, pass/fail results per policy requirement, and specific rewrite suggestions.

---

## Step 1: Access Course Content

The user delivers course content via **Claude in Chrome**. Follow this sequence:

1. Ask the user to open their LearnWorlds course in Chrome (course editor or learner-facing view)
2. Navigate through each lesson/section and read the full page content
3. For each section, extract and count only **qualifying instructional text** (see Word Count Rules below)
4. Record the word count per section before running the full audit

**Alternative inputs accepted:** Pasted text, uploaded `.docx`, or uploaded `.pdf` if Chrome access is unavailable.

---

## Step 2: Word Count Rules
**Source: Policy Section I.4.a + NBCC Tips for Designing a Home Study Program, p. 2**

### What counts toward NBCC credit:
- Instructional prose — the substantive body content of each lesson

### What does NOT count (must be excluded):
- Reference lists and citations
- The program evaluation form
- Learning assessment instruments / post-test questions and answer choices
- Indexes
- Navigation menus, button labels, headers, footers, UI chrome
- Any materials that "do not provide an opportunity for learning"
- Time estimates for self-reflection, journaling, or other participant activities — NBCC does not permit awarding credit for estimated activity completion time
- Transcripts of videos **if** NBCC credit is also being awarded for viewing that video — no double-counting (Policy I.4; NBCC Tips p. 2)

### Credit calculation (Policy I.4.a):
- **1 NBCC hour = 6,000 words** of qualifying instructional text
- Proportionate: 3,000 words = 0.5 hours; 1,500 words = 0.25 hours
- Must be issued in **0.25-hour increments**
- **Minimum: 1,500 words** — any amount less than this does not qualify for NBCC credit
- Formula: Total qualifying words ÷ 6,000 = justified credit hours

### Audio/visual credit (Policy I.4.b) — if applicable:
- 1 NBCC hour = 60 minutes of listening/viewing
- Minimum: 15 minutes; issued in 0.25-hour increments
- Cannot double-count a video and its transcript

## Step 3: Full Compliance Checklist

Evaluate every item. Policy section citations are included for each item.

---

### SECTION A — Program Information & Public Advertising (Policy C.15 + J.4 + J.5)

Before a participant registers, the following must be publicly available:

- [ ] Program title is clearly stated
- [ ] Program content description and learning objectives are published
- [ ] Name and qualifications of each author/presenter are listed
- [ ] Number of NBCC hours offered for completion is stated
- [ ] Provider contact information is published (mailing address, phone, email, website)
- [ ] Required NBCC ACEP Approval Statement appears on all print and electronic materials:
  *"[EduCare Complete] has been approved by NBCC as an Approved Continuing Education Provider, ACEP No. ____. Programs that do not qualify for NBCC credit are clearly identified. [EduCare Complete] is solely responsible for all aspects of the programs."* (Policy J.4.a)
- [ ] NBCC ACEP trademark (logo) is prominently displayed on advertising materials and main website page (Policy J.5)
- [ ] No language claims NBCC "endorses" or "supports" the content or any counseling theory (Policy J.4.b)

---

### SECTION B — Credit Terminology (Policy I.1 + I.4.c)

- [ ] Only permitted terms are used: **NBCC hours, clock hours, credit hours, CE hours, or contact hours**
- [ ] The term **"CEU" is NOT used anywhere** — explicitly prohibited by NBCC policy

---

### SECTION C — Program Content Requirements (Policy D + E + G)

- [ ] Content directly relates to at least one of the 9 NBCC Content Areas (see list in Step 4)
- [ ] The relevant NBCC Content Area(s) are identifiable within the learning objectives (Policy G)
- [ ] Content is directly relevant to the professional knowledge and skills of **graduate-level counselors** (Policy D.3)
- [ ] Content is NOT primarily designed for the general public, paraprofessionals, or non-counseling professions (Policy E.1)
- [ ] Content is NOT primarily concerned with medical or physical health of clients (Policy E.2)
- [ ] Content does NOT promote Sexual Orientation Change Efforts as a therapeutic method (Policy E.4)
- [ ] Content does NOT imply political or religious beliefs as normative or required (Policy E.3)
- [ ] Where content involves diagnosis, evaluation, or treatment of clients — it is clinically sound and consistent with the current DSM edition (Policy D.4)
- [ ] Program materials reflect current information, research, and professional knowledge (Policy D.5)
- [ ] Content is consistent with the NBCC Code of Ethics (Policy D.6)
- [ ] A reference list accurately identifying all source materials (journal articles, books, etc.) is included (Policy J.6.a.5)

---

### SECTION D — Presenter / Author Qualifications (Policy F)

- [ ] All authors/presenters are specifically and accurately identified in program materials with credentials listed (Policy D.7)
- [ ] Presenter qualifies under at least one NBCC category:
  - **Category 1** (required for counseling theory, counseling practice, counseling relationships, and evaluation/treatment of clients): Graduate degree in a mental health field + qualified by education/experience for the subject
  - **Category 2**: Graduate degree directly related to the subject matter + qualified by education/experience
  - **Category 3**: Qualified by appropriate education, experience, and/or training for the subject matter
- [ ] No honorary academic degrees are used to qualify a presenter (Policy F.4.b)
- [ ] Presenter category matches the content being delivered — Category 1 is required for clinical content regardless of presenter's other qualifications

---

### SECTION E — Home Study Program Materials (Policy J.6.a)

The program must contain ALL six of the following:

- [ ] **Learning materials** — text-based, audio/visual, or web-based content (J.6.a.1)
- [ ] **Author/presenter identification** — names, credentials, and qualifications of each (J.6.a.2)
- [ ] **Assessment instrument** — prepared by a professional with an advanced degree in a mental health field; must evaluate the participant's knowledge of program material (quiz or test) (J.6.a.3)
- [ ] **Instructions for obtaining NBCC credit** — clearly explains the process upon completion (J.6.a.4)
- [ ] **Reference list** — accurately identifies all source materials used to prepare the program (J.6.a.5)
- [ ] **Participant evaluation document** — allows participants to rate satisfaction per Policy H.1 (J.6.a.6)

---

### SECTION F — Participant Evaluation Requirements (Policy H.1)

The evaluation must:

- [ ] Be available to every participant in print or electronic form (H.1)
- [ ] Include a rating scale (H.1)
- [ ] Identify the title and date of the program (H.1)
- [ ] Allow the participant to indicate satisfaction or dissatisfaction with:
  - [ ] The **content** of the program (H.1.a)
  - [ ] The **knowledge and presentation skills** of each presenter (H.1.b)
  - [ ] The **content and quality of program materials** (H.1.d)
  - [ ] The **relevance of the program** to professional counselors (H.1.e)
  - *(Note: facility/accommodations H.1.c applies to live programs only — not required for home study)*
- [ ] Allow the participant to submit **written comments** and indicate whether the program met their expectations (H.1)
- [ ] Evaluations are maintained by EduCare for at least **5 years** from the date of each program (H.1)

---

### SECTION G — Learning Assessment (Policy I.4.c + J.6.a.3 + NBCC Tips p. 2)

- [ ] A learning assessment (quiz/test) is present
- [ ] Assessment is completed **before** the certificate of completion is issued (I.4.c)
- [ ] Assessment is designed to demonstrate learning and program completion — not just attendance (NBCC Tips p. 2)
- [ ] Assessment was prepared by a professional with an **advanced degree in a mental health field** (J.6.a.3)
- [ ] Time spent completing the assessment does NOT count toward NBCC credit hours (NBCC Tips p. 1)

---

### SECTION H — Certificate of Completion (Policy I.5)

The certificate or letter must include ALL of the following (Policy I.5.a–f):

- [ ] Name and contact information of EduCare Complete (I.5.a)
- [ ] Title and **date of completion** of the home study program (I.5.b)
- [ ] **Name of the participant** to whom NBCC hours are awarded (I.5.c)
- [ ] **Number of NBCC hours** awarded to the named participant (I.5.d)
- [ ] Name and **signature of an authorized representative** of EduCare Complete (I.5.e)
- [ ] **NBCC ACEP number** of the provider (I.5.f)
- [ ] Certificate is made available **only after** completion — not before the program is finished (I.5)

---

### SECTION I — Program Quality & Functionality (Policy J.6.b + C.8 + C.9 + NBCC Tips p. 2)

- [ ] All program materials are of **professional quality** in content and appearance (J.6.b)
- [ ] All materials are fully functional and accessible to participants and NBCC (J.6.b)
- [ ] Materials do not infringe on copyright, trademark, or license rights (J.6.b + C.9)
- [ ] A procedure exists for **systematically reviewing** the program to keep content current (C.8; NBCC Tips p. 2)
- [ ] If recorded content is used, participants are not identifiable by name or image unless consent was obtained (C.11; NBCC Tips p. 2)
- [ ] Electronic payments (if used) are processed through **secure encryption** (J.6.c)
- [ ] The LMS (LearnWorlds) allows customization of each component to satisfy all Policy requirements (NBCC Tips p. 2)

---

### SECTION J — Record-Keeping Requirements (Policy C.10)

EduCare must maintain the following for at least **5 years** per program:

- [ ] Roster of all program participants (C.10.a)
- [ ] List of all home study authors/presenters with qualifications and content descriptions (C.10.c)
- [ ] Program content description (C.10.d)
- [ ] Participant evaluations (C.10.e)
- [ ] Promotional and marketing materials (C.10.f)
- [ ] Program materials distributed to participants, including learning objectives (C.10.g)

---

## Step 4: NBCC Content Areas (Policy Section G)

Course content must directly and primarily relate to at least one. The relevant area(s) must be identifiable in the learning objectives.

1. Counseling Theory/Practice and the Counseling Relationship *(Category 1 Presenter required)*
2. Human Growth and Development
3. Social and Cultural Foundations
4. Group Dynamics and Counseling *(Category 1 Presenter required)*
5. Career Development and Counseling
6. Assessment *(Category 1 Presenter required for clinical assessment topics)*
7. Research and Program Evaluation
8. Counselor Professional Identity and Practice Issues
9. Wellness and Prevention

## Important Auditor Notes

- **Do not count anything that does not provide a direct learning opportunity.** When in doubt, exclude it.
- **Video without transcript:** Flag as ⚠️ PARTIAL for text credit — on-demand video earns audio/visual credit (60 min = 1 hr), not text credit. Do not award both for the same content.
- **Pre-approval courses:** If EduCare does not yet have an ACEP number, note that the provider number field contains a placeholder and flag certificate and approval statement language for review before submission to NBCC.
- **Presenter category mismatch:** Any course content involving counseling theory, counseling practice, counseling relationships, or evaluation/treatment of clients requires a Category 1 Presenter. Flag this even if the presenter holds strong credentials in another category.
- **LMS functionality:** Confirm that LearnWorlds is configured to deliver all required components (evaluation, assessment, certificate) in the correct sequence — assessment must precede certificate issuance.
- **Always anchor rewrite suggestions** to the specific NBCC Content Area the course covers, so any new content serves both word count and content alignment compliance simultaneously.
